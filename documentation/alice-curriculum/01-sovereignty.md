# Level 1: What Is Sovereignty?

## Core Concept
Sovereignty is the ability to own your data, your keys, and your choices. It means you don't rent your life from someone else.

## What You Learn

Most of us live in borrowed houses. We use Gmail, Slack, Dropbox — and they own the foundation. Our data, our conversations, our identity all live on someone else's servers, governed by their rules. If they change the rules, get hacked, or shut down, we have no say.

Sovereignty is different. It means you own the root. Your data lives on *your* machines, governed by *your* code, protected by *your* keys.

This isn't about building a bunker or living off-grid. It's about having agency. It's about knowing that your most important relationships, your work, your knowledge — they're *yours* to control.

koad:io is built around this idea. It gives ordinary people the tools to be sovereigns in their own digital lives.

## What You Do

Take 10 minutes and think about:
- One thing you do online every single day (email, calendar, chat, files)
- Where it actually lives (which company's servers)
- What would happen if that company changed its terms, deleted your account, or went under

Write down your answer. No perfect answer — just honest reflection.

## What You Have at the End

- A clear question: *What do I actually own right now?*
- The beginning of a vision: that you could own more
- Readiness to take the next step
